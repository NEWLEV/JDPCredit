FAQ

Common questions about credit repair

Do you offer free credit consulting in Miami?

Yes — JDP offers completely FREE credit consulting with no obligation. We'll review your situation, explain your options, and give you an honest plan. Call (786) 520-5461 or fill out the form above.

How does the credit dispute process work?

We pull and evaluate your reports from all three bureaus, identify potentially inaccurate, erroneous, false, or obsolete items, and you decide what to dispute. We prepare correspondence and support you at every stage.

How long does credit repair take?

Timelines vary. Bureaus typically have 30–45 days per dispute cycle. Most clients see responses within 30–90 days. Complex files may take 6–12 months.

What items can be disputed or removed?

Under the FCRA, you have the right to dispute any item that is inaccurate, erroneous, false, or obsolete — including incorrectly reported late payments, duplicate accounts, outdated collections, incorrect balances, fraudulent accounts, and unverifiable negative items.

Is there a contract or cancellation fee?

No long-term contracts for the monthly plan. Cancel anytime. Annual and Business packages have their own terms — ask during your free consultation.

What is your money-back guarantee?

Can you help me build business credit and get funding?

Absolutely. We help you structure your entity (EIN, DUNS, business bank account), build a profile with Dun & Bradstreet, Equifax Business, and Experian Business, dispute incorrect items, and identify the right funding sources.

Credit Repair in Miami: A Complete Guide

Service: Credit Repair | Updated: 2025 | Miami, FL

If you're searching for credit repair in Miami, you're not alone. Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), you have the legal right to dispute any information on your credit report that is inaccurate, erroneous, false, or obsolete.

What Can Be Disputed?

  • Late payments reported incorrectly
  • Collections and charge-offs that are inaccurate or unverifiable
  • Duplicate accounts reported by multiple creditors
  • Fraudulent accounts opened without consent
  • Hard inquiries made without authorization
  • Outdated negative items older than 7–10 years

How Credit Repair Works at JDP

We begin with a free consultation, then conduct a thorough credit audit of all three bureaus, create a dispute plan, prepare correspondence, and guide you through every bureau response. Throughout the process, you receive credit education for long-term habits.

Credit Repair Laws

The CROA requires written contracts, a three-day cancellation right, and prohibits charging before services are performed. JDP operates in full compliance with CROA, FCRA, and all consumer protection laws.

Business Credit & Funding: Build Credit That Gets You Funded

Service: Business Credit & Funding | Updated: 2025

Business credit is separate from personal credit and is used by lenders, vendors, and investors to evaluate your company.

Why Business Credit Matters

  • Qualify for business loans and SBA funding
  • Access net-30 vendor accounts
  • Secure higher limits and lower rates
  • Protect personal credit from business debt

Building Business Credit Step by Step

  • Step 1: Establish business identity (EIN, LLC/Corp)
  • Step 2: Open a dedicated business bank account
  • Step 3: Register with Dun & Bradstreet (DUNS number)
  • Step 4: Open starter vendor/trade accounts
  • Step 5: Apply for business credit cards
  • Step 6: Layer in bank credit as your profile matures

What Is a Credit Audit and Why Do You Need One?

Service: Credit Audit | Updated: 2025

A credit audit is a comprehensive review of your reports from all three bureaus. Studies suggest approximately 1 in 5 Americans has an error on at least one report.

What a Credit Audit Reviews

  • Payment history accuracy
  • Account balances and limits
  • Negative items (collections, charge-offs)
  • Hard inquiries
  • Public records
  • Personal information errors

We recommend a credit audit at least once per year, and immediately before any major financial decision.

Credit Education: Understanding Your Score to Keep It High

Service: Credit Education | Updated: 2025

JDP's credit education program teaches you exactly how credit scores work so your gains become permanent.

The 5 Score Factors

  • Payment History (35%) — one late payment can drop 50–100 points
  • Credit Utilization (30%) — keep below 30%, ideally under 10%
  • Length of History (15%) — don't close old cards
  • Credit Mix (10%) — revolving + installment
  • New Inquiries (10%) — each hard inquiry: 5–10 point drop

Credit-Building Strategies

  • Becoming an authorized user on a long-standing account
  • Opening a secured credit card
  • Using a credit-builder loan
  • Setting up autopay for minimums
  • Requesting credit limit increases
